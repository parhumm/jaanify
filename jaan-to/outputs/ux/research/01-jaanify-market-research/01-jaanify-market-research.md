## Recommendations

### CRITICAL: Design the 3-Tier Reasoning Card as the Core Trust Engine

**INSIGHT**: Users need transparency but will disengage from information overload. The inverted U-curve means the design of transparency is as important as the transparency itself.

**SO WHAT**: If Jaanify gets the transparency level wrong — either too much or too little — the entire product thesis fails. 84% demand for explainability becomes 0% adoption if the explanation overwhelms.

**NOW WHAT**: Implement progressive disclosure with these exact tiers: Tier 1 (one-line, <=80 chars, always visible), Tier 2 (factor weights with visual bars, tap to expand), Tier 3 (full reasoning chain, available on demand). Default to Tier 1. A/B test Tier 1 format (1-line vs 2-line vs icon) in first 2 weeks post-launch.

**SUCCESS METRIC**: Reasoning Card expand rate >30% (Tier 1→Tier 2); AI Transparency Satisfaction >4.0/5.0 in 7-day NPS survey.

### CRITICAL: Achieve First AI-Enriched Task in Under 30 Seconds

**INSIGHT**: The Day 30 retention cliff (9.63%) is caused by slow time-to-value, not missing features.
